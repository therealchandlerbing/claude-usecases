# Interview Question Templates

Research planning guides and question templates for persona validation.

---

## Overview

Structured interview questions ensure complete framework coverage and yield evidence for high-quality personas. These templates align with Vianeo four-layer structure, Jobs-to-be-Done, and empathy mapping frameworks.

---

## Vianeo Four-Layer Questions

### Layer 1: Requester (Who They Are)

**Life/Motivations:**
- "Tell me about your role. What does a typical week look like?"
- "What originally drew you to this field/position?"
- "What does success look like in your role?"
- "What keeps you motivated when things get difficult?"
- "How do you measure whether you're having a good year?"

**Personality/Values:**
- "How do you typically approach making important decisions?"
- "What's more important to you: moving fast or getting it right?"
- "How do you handle uncertainty or incomplete information?"
- "What would colleagues say about your working style?"
- "What frustrates you most about how others work?"

### Layer 2: Field of Application (Their World)

**Thinks/Feels:**
- "What keeps you up at night about [domain]?"
- "What are you most hopeful about in the next year?"
- "What concerns do you have that you might not share publicly?"
- "How confident do you feel about [specific area]?"

**Observes:**
- "What patterns have you noticed in [domain] recently?"
- "What's changing in your environment that concerns or excites you?"
- "What do you see others doing that works well or poorly?"
- "What trends are you tracking?"

**Does:**
- "Walk me through how you handled [recent specific situation]."
- "How do you currently approach [specific task]?"
- "What's your process for [relevant activity]?"
- "How do you spend most of your time?"

**Others Say:**
- "What feedback do you get from your team/partners/stakeholders?"
- "How do others describe your approach to [domain]?"
- "What do people come to you for advice about?"
- "What criticism have you received that stuck with you?"

### Layer 3: Activities and Challenges

**Tasks/Activities:**
- "What are your main responsibilities in [domain]?"
- "What tasks take up most of your time?"
- "What processes do you own or manage?"
- "What decisions do you make regularly?"

**Pains/Lacks:**
- "What's the most frustrating part of [task/domain]?"
- "Where do you feel you're wasting time or effort?"
- "What do you wish you could do but can't?"
- "How much time/money does [specific pain] cost you?"
- "What have you tried that didn't work?"

**Expectations/Hopes:**
- "If [problem] were solved, what would be different?"
- "What would success look like in an ideal world?"
- "What outcomes are you trying to achieve?"
- "How would you know if things were working better?"

### Layer 4: Current Solutions

**Current Solutions:**
- "What do you currently use for [need]?"
- "How did you choose that solution?"
- "What works well about your current approach?"
- "What doesn't work well? What's missing?"
- "What have you tried before that you stopped using? Why?"
- "What workarounds have you developed?"

## Jobs-to-be-Done Questions

### Functional Jobs
- "When you [behavior], what are you trying to accomplish?"
- "What tasks need to get done that you struggle with?"
- "Walk me through the last time you [relevant activity]."
- "What does 'done' look like for [task]?"

### Emotional Jobs
- "How do you want to feel when [task/situation] is handled well?"
- "What emotions come up when dealing with [domain]?"
- "What would make you feel confident about [area]?"
- "What frustrations do you experience around [task]?"

### Social Jobs
- "How do you want others to see you in [context]?"
- "What would make you look good to [stakeholders]?"
- "What image do you want to project?"
- "What do you want your team/peers/leadership to think of you?"

### Pain Points
- "What obstacles prevent you from [desired outcome]?"
- "What risks worry you about [task/decision]?"
- "What makes [task] harder than it should be?"
- "What negative outcomes are you trying to avoid?"

### Gains
- "What would make [task] delightful instead of just done?"
- "What would exceed your expectations?"
- "What benefits are most important to you?"
- "What would make this a major success?"

## Empathy Mapping Questions

### Says
- "How would you describe [situation] to a colleague?"
- "What's the most common complaint you hear about [domain]?"
- "What do you tell people about your experience with [area]?"
- "What advice do you give others about [topic]?"

### Thinks
- "What's really on your mind about [situation]?"
- "What assumptions are you operating under?"
- "What do you wonder about that you can't easily answer?"
- "What's the real issue behind [surface problem]?"

### Does
- "What did you actually do in [recent situation]?"
- "How do you typically respond to [challenge]?"
- "What steps do you take when [trigger]?"
- "What's your first action when [situation]?"

### Feels
- "How do you feel when [situation occurs]?"
- "What emotional reaction do you have to [domain/task]?"
- "What energizes or drains you about [area]?"
- "What's your gut reaction to [topic]?"

## Persona-Type Specific Questions

### Partner Personas
- "How do you evaluate potential partnerships?"
- "What makes a partnership successful vs. unsuccessful?"
- "How do collaboration decisions get made in your organization?"
- "What's your communication preference with partners?"
- "What has caused partnerships to fail in your experience?"
- "How long does it typically take to move from interest to agreement?"

### Innovator Personas
- "What stage is your technology at?"
- "What validation have you completed so far?"
- "What resources or capabilities do you lack?"
- "How do you prefer to receive feedback and support?"
- "What's your timeline for key milestones?"
- "What obstacles are blocking your progress?"

### Stakeholder Personas
- "What information do you need to make decisions?"
- "What triggers you to say yes or no?"
- "How do you evaluate risk vs. opportunity?"
- "What would make you champion something internally?"
- "What causes you to lose confidence in a project?"
- "How do you prefer to receive updates and reports?"

### Beneficiary Personas
- "How does [problem] affect your daily life?"
- "What have you tried to address [need]?"
- "What would be different if [problem] were solved?"
- "What concerns would you have about [potential solution]?"
- "What would make you trust [new approach]?"
- "How would you know if something was actually helping?"

### Customer/Buyer Personas
- "How did you first realize you had this need?"
- "What options did you consider?"
- "Who else was involved in the decision?"
- "What would have made you choose differently?"
- "What objections or concerns did you have?"
- "What made you finally decide to move forward?"

## Interview Best Practices

### Before the Interview
1. Review any existing data about this stakeholder type
2. Identify specific gaps to fill
3. Prepare follow-up questions for likely responses
4. Set up recording (with permission)
5. Plan for 45-60 minutes

### During the Interview
1. Start with easy context questions
2. Ask about specific recent examples, not hypotheticals
3. Follow interesting threads—the script is a guide
4. Dig into "why" and "how much/how often"
5. Document exact quotes when notable
6. Note observable behaviors and reactions

### Follow-Up Probes
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What did that cost you in time/money/effort?"
- "Why do you think that is?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What happened next?"
- "What would ideal look like?"

### After the Interview
1. Complete notes within 24 hours while memory is fresh
2. Tag quotes with layer/framework element
3. Note validation status (direct vs. inferred)
4. Identify gaps for follow-up
5. Update persona segments if patterns shift

## Research Planning Guidelines

### Sample Size by Target Score

| Target Score | Interviews per Persona | Total for 3 Personas |
|--------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Score 5 | 10+ | 30+ |
| Score 4 | 5-10 | 15-30 |
| Score 3 | 3-5 | 9-15 |
| Score 2 | 1-2 | 3-6 |

### Recruitment Criteria
- Represent target segment demographics
- Recent experience with relevant activities
- Willingness to share honestly
- Mix of positive and negative experiences
- Diverse perspectives within segment

### Timeline Planning

**Quick research (Score 3):**
- Week 1: Recruit 9-15 participants
- Week 2-3: Conduct interviews
- Week 4: Synthesize and generate personas

**Thorough research (Score 4-5):**
- Week 1-2: Recruit 15-30+ participants
- Week 2-5: Conduct interviews
- Week 6: Synthesize and generate personas
- Week 7: Validate with stakeholder review

## Sample Interview Script

### Introduction (5 minutes)
"Thank you for taking the time to speak with me. I'm researching [topic] to better understand [stakeholder type] experiences. There are no right or wrong answers—I'm interested in your honest perspective. I'll be taking notes, and with your permission, recording this conversation. The recording will only be used for accurate note-taking. Is that okay?"

### Warm-Up (5 minutes)
"Let's start with some background. Can you tell me about your role and what a typical week looks like?"

[Layer 1 questions about Life/Motivations]

### Context and World (15 minutes)
"Now I'd like to understand more about how you approach [domain]."

[Layer 2 questions: Thinks/Feels, Observes, Does, Others Say]

### Activities and Challenges (15 minutes)
"Let's talk about the specific tasks and challenges you face."

[Layer 3 questions: Tasks, Pains, Expectations]

### Current Reality (10 minutes)
"I'd like to understand what you're currently using and how it's working."

[Layer 4 questions: Current Solutions]

### Closing (5 minutes)
"Is there anything else you think I should know about [topic] that we haven't covered?"

"Would it be okay to follow up if I have additional questions?"

"Thank you so much for your time and insights."
