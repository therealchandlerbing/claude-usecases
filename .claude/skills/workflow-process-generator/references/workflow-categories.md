# Workflow Categories Guide

Detailed descriptions of the five workflow categories at 360 Social Impact Studios.

---

## Overview

360 operates five distinct workflow types. Each has unique characteristics that inform visualization approach and data extraction strategy.

---

## 1. Partnership Development

### Pattern
**Relationship building → scoping → formalization → integration**

### Duration
**2-12 months** (varies by geography and complexity)

### Key Characteristics
- Heavy emphasis on relationship and cultural alignment
- Long timeline with unpredictable pace (especially international)
- Multiple decision gates throughout process
- Legal review often a bottleneck (4-8 weeks)
- High exit rate early (40% at initial contact) but strong mid-funnel (85% from scoping to formalization)

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Pursue Partnership?** (After Initial Contact)
- **Location:** End of Phase 1
- **Criteria:** Mission alignment + capability fit + resource availability
- **Historical Data:** 60% proceed, 40% polite decline
- **Owner:** Business Development

**Decision 2: Ready for Scoping?** (After Relationship Building)
- **Location:** End of Phase 2
- **Criteria:** Trust established + clear collaboration areas + stakeholder buy-in
- **Historical Data:** 70% proceed to scoping, 15% continue building, 15% exit
- **Owner:** Partnership Lead

**Decision 3: Structure Selection** (During Scoping)
- **Location:** Phase 3 (Scoping Workshop)
- **Options:** Joint Venture | Licensing | Distribution | Strategic Alliance
- **Criteria:** IP ownership + revenue sharing + resource investment + time horizon
- **Owner:** Joint Working Group

**Decision 4: Terms Acceptable?** (After Legal Review)
- **Location:** End of Phase 4
- **Criteria:** Legal risk acceptable + business terms viable + internal approvals secured
- **Historical Data:** 85% accept, 10% renegotiate, 5% exit
- **Owner:** Legal Teams + Executives

### Common Data Sources

**Asana:**
- Partnership development project templates
- Relationship tracking tasks
- Custom fields: Stage, Partner Type, Geography, Structure

**Drive:**
- Partnership agreements
- MOUs and term sheets
- Collaboration frameworks
- Cultural context documentation

**Gmail:**
- Threads containing "partnership," "collaboration," "joint venture"
- International relationship building correspondence
- Scoping workshop coordination
- Legal review discussions

### Visualization Approach
- **Diagram Type:** Flowchart (decision-heavy)
- **Emphasis:** Timeline ranges, cultural considerations, decision gates
- **Color Coding:** Primary path for standard progression, exits clearly marked in red
- **Special Elements:** Show geographic variations (Brazil: +2-4 weeks for relationship building)

### Example Partnerships
- GenIP (technology transfer partnership)
- SpacePlan (design services collaboration)
- CNEN (nuclear assessment partnership)
- Brazil university partnerships

## 2. Client Engagement

### Pattern
**Inquiry → qualification → proposal → execution → delivery**

### Duration
**1-6 months** (varies by scope and service tier)

### Key Characteristics
- High-volume workflow (100+ inquiries annually)
- Clear conversion funnel with measurable drop-off
- Three service tier paths: Fast Track (90min) / Standard (4-5h) / Complete (6-7h)
- 42% overall conversion from inquiry to completion
- 86% high satisfaction rate
- Strong qualification process (60% pass initial qualification)

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Qualification Pass?** (After Initial Assessment)
- **Location:** End of Phase 2
- **Criteria:** Budget available + timeline realistic + scope fits expertise + decision-maker engaged
- **Historical Data:** 60% qualified, 40% declined or referred
- **Owner:** Business Development

**Decision 2: Service Tier Selection** (During Proposal)
- **Location:** Phase 3
- **Options:** Fast Track | Standard | Complete
- **Criteria:** Client budget + timeline + depth needed + resource availability
- **Distribution:** Fast Track 20%, Standard 60%, Complete 20%
- **Owner:** Client (with 360 recommendation)

**Decision 3: Proposal Acceptance** (After Client Review)
- **Location:** End of Phase 4
- **Criteria:** Client approval + budget confirmed + timeline agreed
- **Historical Data:** 75% of proposals accepted
- **Owner:** Client

### Common Data Sources

**Asana:**
- Client project templates with custom fields for stage tracking
- Service tier templates (Fast Track / Standard / Complete)
- Task completion rates and durations

**Drive:**
- Proposals and SOWs
- Client deliverables
- Assessment reports
- Service tier descriptions

**Gmail:**
- Client correspondence
- Proposal discussions
- Requirement gathering emails
- Project kickoff coordination

### Visualization Approach
- **Diagram Type:** Flowchart or Sankey (for volume emphasis)
- **Emphasis:** Conversion metrics at each stage, service tier paths
- **Color Coding:**
  - Fast Track: Primary green
  - Standard: Alternative terracotta
  - Complete: Enhanced plum
- **Special Elements:** Show volume percentages and conversion rates prominently

### Example Metrics
- 42% conversion from inquiry to completion
- 86% high satisfaction rate
- 8-phase workflow from inquiry through delivery
- Average project duration: Fast Track (2 weeks), Standard (6 weeks), Complete (12 weeks)

## 3. Innovation Assessment

### Pattern
**Intake → evaluation → prioritization → validation → recommendations**

### Duration
**2-8 weeks** (varies by portfolio size and depth)

### Key Characteristics
- Two primary methodologies: 360 Innovation Compass vs Vianeo Business Validation
- Three execution depths: Fast Track / Standard / Complete (similar to client engagement)
- Evidence-based evaluation with scoring rubrics
- Threshold gates determine advancement
- University TTO partnerships common (technology transfer context)

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Methodology Selection** (After Intake)
- **Location:** Beginning of Phase 2
- **Options:** 360 Innovation Compass | Vianeo Business Validation
- **Criteria:**
  - 360 Compass: Rapid innovation screening, early-stage tech
  - Vianeo: Comprehensive business model validation, market-ready innovations
- **Owner:** 360 Assessment Team

**Decision 2: Execution Depth** (During Scoping)
- **Location:** Phase 2
- **Options:** Fast Track (90 min) | Standard (4-5 hours) | Complete (6-7 hours)
- **Criteria:** Client budget + portfolio size + timeline
- **Owner:** Client (with 360 recommendation)

**Decision 3: Advancement Threshold** (After Evaluation)
- **Location:** End of evaluation phases
- **Criteria:** Scoring thresholds (varies by methodology)
  - Vianeo: LDAFV scores (Legitimacy, Desirability, Acceptability, Feasibility, Viability)
  - 360 Compass: Innovation readiness score
- **Owner:** Assessment Team

### Common Data Sources

**Asana:**
- Assessment project templates
- Custom evaluation fields
- Scoring rubrics
- Portfolio tracking

**Drive:**
- Framework documentation (360 Compass, Vianeo)
- Scoring rubrics
- Final assessment reports
- Portfolio analyses
- University TTO collaboration docs

**Gmail:**
- University TTO communications
- Inventor discussions
- Stakeholder coordination
- Framework selection discussions

### Visualization Approach
- **Diagram Type:** Decision tree or parallel paths (for service tiers)
- **Emphasis:** Methodology branches, evidence requirements, threshold gates
- **Color Coding:** Different colors for each service tier path
- **Special Elements:** Show Vianeo 10-step workflow as gold standard, include parallel evaluation dimensions (LDAFV)

### Example Assessments
- CNEN Nuclear Technology Portfolio (Vianeo Complete)
- University TTO Innovation Screening (360 Compass Standard)
- Corporate Innovation Pipeline Evaluation

### Reference Standard
**Vianeo Business Model Evaluation** is the gold standard for this category. 10-step workflow with three execution paths showing:
- Time investments per step
- Prerequisites and outputs
- Evidence requirements
- Quality gates
- Professional interactive visualization

## 4. Ecosystem Mapping

### Pattern
**Discovery → stakeholder analysis → relationship mapping → opportunity identification**

### Duration
**3-12 weeks** (varies by geography and ecosystem complexity)

### Key Characteristics
- Network-based visualization (not linear flow)
- Five value network positions: Enablers → Providers → Channels → Buyers → End Users
- Stakeholder categorization by type and acceptability (favorable/neutral/unfavorable)
- Geographic focus (Brazil, Seattle, specific sectors)
- Relationship discovery and connector identification

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Scope Boundaries** (Initial Planning)
- **Location:** Phase 1
- **Criteria:** Geography + sector + stakeholder types to include
- **Options:** Regional (Seattle, Brazil) | Sectoral (clean tech, nuclear) | Thematic (innovation ecosystem)
- **Owner:** Project Lead

**Decision 2: Stakeholder Prioritization** (After Discovery)
- **Location:** Phase 2
- **Criteria:** Relevance + influence + accessibility + alignment
- **Owner:** Ecosystem Team

**Decision 3: Engagement Depth** (After Mapping)
- **Location:** Phase 3
- **Criteria:** Resource investment + strategic importance + relationship strength
- **Owner:** Business Development

### Common Data Sources

**Asana:**
- Regional ecosystem projects (Brazil, Seattle)
- Sector-specific ecosystem mapping
- Stakeholder contact tracking

**Drive:**
- Stakeholder maps
- Ecosystem visualizations
- Network analyses
- Regional reports
- Connector relationship documentation

**Gmail:**
- Introduction threads
- Connector conversations
- Stakeholder outreach
- Ecosystem event coordination

### Visualization Approach
- **Diagram Type:** Network diagram or Sankey (for value flows)
- **Emphasis:** Relationship flows, stakeholder categories, value network positions
- **Color Coding:** By acceptability (green=favorable, yellow=neutral, orange=unfavorable)
- **Special Elements:** Show 5 value network positions, use Sankey for value flow across network

### Example Projects
- Seattle Innovation Ecosystem Mapping
- Brazil Tech Transfer Network Analysis
- Nuclear Technology Stakeholder Landscape
- Clean Energy Partnership Ecosystem

## 5. Internal Operations

### Pattern
**Planning → execution → review → optimization**

### Duration
**Varies by operation type** (weekly to quarterly cycles)

### Key Characteristics
- Recurring patterns (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Heavy on dependencies and handoffs
- Resource allocation and capacity planning
- Multi-stakeholder coordination
- Process optimization opportunities

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Resource Allocation** (Planning Phase)
- **Location:** Beginning of cycle
- **Criteria:** Priority + capacity + deadline + strategic importance
- **Owner:** Leadership Team

**Decision 2: Timeline Commitments** (Planning Phase)
- **Location:** Phase 1
- **Criteria:** Capacity availability + dependency alignment + deadline requirements
- **Owner:** Project Leads

**Decision 3: Priority Sequencing** (Execution Phase)
- **Location:** Throughout execution
- **Criteria:** Urgency + importance + dependencies + resource availability
- **Owner:** Operations Lead

**Decision 4: Process Optimization Trigger** (Review Phase)
- **Location:** End of cycle
- **Criteria:** Bottlenecks identified + efficiency opportunity + resource impact
- **Owner:** Process Owner

### Common Data Sources

**Asana:**
- Board prep projects
- Contractor coordination tasks
- Operational templates
- Team protocols
- Sprint planning boards

**Drive:**
- Board materials
- Operational SOPs
- Team protocols
- Planning documents
- Retrospective notes

**Gmail:**
- Internal coordination threads
- Team alignment discussions
- Process improvement discussions
- Resource allocation emails

### Visualization Approach
- **Diagram Type:** Gantt timeline or swimlane diagram
- **Emphasis:** Dependencies, handoffs, resource allocation
- **Color Coding:** By team or by priority
- **Special Elements:** Show parallel tracks for different teams/workstreams

### Example Operations
- Quarterly Board Preparation Workflow
- Weekly Sprint Planning Process
- Monthly Newsletter Production
- Contractor Onboarding Process
- Quarterly Strategic Review Cycle

## Category Selection Decision Tree

**To Identify the Right Category:**

```
Q1: Is this about external partnerships?
  → YES: Partnership Development
  → NO: Continue

Q2: Is this about client services?
  → YES: Client Engagement
  → NO: Continue

Q3: Is this about evaluating innovations/technologies?
  → YES: Innovation Assessment
  → NO: Continue

Q4: Is this about mapping stakeholders/ecosystem?
  → YES: Ecosystem Mapping
  → NO: Likely Internal Operations

Q5: Is this about internal team processes?
  → YES: Internal Operations
  → NO: Check categories again or create custom workflow
```

## Cross-Category Patterns

### Common Elements Across All Categories

1. **Intake/Discovery Phase** - Every workflow starts with information gathering
2. **Decision Gates** - Most workflows have 2-4 major decision points
3. **Execution Phase(s)** - Core work happens in middle phases
4. **Delivery/Completion** - Every workflow ends with output delivery
5. **Review/Optimization** - Mature workflows include improvement cycles

### Unique Elements by Category

| Category | Unique Elements |
|----------|----------------|
| Partnership | Cultural considerations, legal review, relationship building emphasis |
| Client | Service tiers, qualification funnel, satisfaction metrics |
| Assessment | Methodology selection, scoring rubrics, evidence requirements |
| Ecosystem | Network visualization, stakeholder categorization, value flow mapping |
| Internal | Recurring cycles, resource allocation, team dependencies |

## Workflow Category Metadata

### Partnership Development
- **Typical Duration:** 2-12 months
- **Volume:** 10-20 partnerships explored annually
- **Success Rate:** 35% (from initial contact to formalized partnership)
- **Primary Bottleneck:** Legal review (4-8 weeks)
- **Best Practice:** Cultural context gathering for international partnerships

### Client Engagement
- **Typical Duration:** 1-6 months
- **Volume:** 100+ inquiries annually
- **Success Rate:** 42% conversion (inquiry to project completion)
- **Primary Bottleneck:** Qualification (careful screening prevents downstream issues)
- **Best Practice:** Clear service tier definitions upfront

### Innovation Assessment
- **Typical Duration:** 2-8 weeks
- **Volume:** 20-40 assessments annually
- **Success Rate:** 90%+ completion (pre-qualified through partnerships)
- **Primary Bottleneck:** Evidence gathering for comprehensive assessments
- **Best Practice:** Methodology selection guide based on innovation stage

### Ecosystem Mapping
- **Typical Duration:** 3-12 weeks
- **Volume:** 4-8 major mapping projects annually
- **Success Rate:** 100% (research-based, no "failure")
- **Primary Bottleneck:** Stakeholder interview scheduling
- **Best Practice:** Leverage connectors for relationship introductions

### Internal Operations
- **Typical Duration:** Varies (weekly to quarterly)
- **Volume:** Continuous
- **Success Rate:** Varies by process
- **Primary Bottleneck:** Resource constraints and competing priorities
- **Best Practice:** Clear dependency mapping and handoff documentation
