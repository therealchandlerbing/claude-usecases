## Overview

The Contract Redlining Tool is an automated contract review and analysis system that produces attorney-quality redlines with tracked changes, margin comments, and comprehensive negotiation guidance. Built from 360's actual contract templates and negotiation patterns, this skill applies institutional knowledge to protect 360's interests while building strong partnerships.

## Purpose

Transform contract review from a time-consuming, attorney-dependent process into a fast, consistent, and strategic capability. The tool identifies risks, generates professional redlines, and provides negotiation guidance tailored to deal type, power dynamics, and cultural context.

## 360's Standard Positions

### Client Services Contracts
- **Payment:** 50% upfront, 50% at milestone | Net 30 terms
- **Scope:** Specific deliverables with 1-2 revision rounds
- **IP:** Client owns deliverables, 360 keeps methodologies
- **Liability:** Cap at fees paid, no consequential damages
- **Termination:** 30-60 days notice, payment for work done

### Partnership Agreements
- **Commission:** 25-30% (30% software, 25% services)
- **Payment:** Only after 360 receives customer payment
- **Exclusivity:** Conditional on first sale + performance minimums
- **Non-Compete:** 12 months post-termination, narrowly defined
- **Prospect Protection:** 6 months (renewable if active)

### Memoranda of Understanding
- **Nature:** Non-binding, no financial commitments
- **Confidentiality:** Mutual, survives termination
- **Publicity:** General announcement okay, deal terms need approval
- **Term:** 1 year auto-renew with 30-day notice
- **Governing Law:** Brazil for Brazilian partners, Washington for U.S.

## Non-Negotiables

360 will **not** accept:
- IP assignment of methodologies, frameworks, or tools
- Unlimited liability without caps
- Payment contingent on subjective "satisfaction"
- One-sided termination without payment for completed work
- Equity without governance/information rights
- Automatic renewal without exit rights

These are deal-breakers. Walk away if the partner insists.

## Common Use Cases

### Scenario 1: Brazilian Partnership
**Context:** MoU with Brazilian biotech, exploring collaboration on innovation initiatives

**Issues Detected:**
- Governing law as Washington State (should be Brazil)
- No arbitration clause (expensive cross-border litigation)
- Vague publicity rights (could leak sensitive deal terms)

**Redlines Applied:**
- Change to Brazilian governing law with CCBC arbitration in S√£o Paulo
- Add PTAX currency conversion language
- Distinguish general publicity (allowed) from deal specifics (requires approval)

**Outcome:** Professional redlines that show respect for partner's jurisdiction while protecting 360's interests

### Scenario 2: Client Services Overreach
**Context:** $26K services contract with unlimited revisions and subjective approval

**Issues Detected:**
- Payment contingent on "satisfactory deliverables" (subjective)
- "Revisions as needed" (unlimited free work)
- Net 60 payment terms (cash flow issue)

**Redlines Applied:**
- Tie payment to delivery with 10-day acceptance window
- Limit to 1-2 revision rounds, price additional rounds
- Reduce to Net 30 with late payment interest

**Outcome:** Protected against scope creep while maintaining good client relationship

### Scenario 3: Partnership Exclusivity
**Context:** New Latin America partner wants immediate exclusive rights

**Issues Detected:**
- Day-1 exclusivity without proving capability
- Covers entire continent (massive geographic lock-up)
- No performance requirements or review process

**Redlines Applied:**
- Change to conditional exclusivity requiring first sale
- Narrow geography to Brazil only
- Add performance minimums and quarterly reviews
- Right to terminate exclusivity while continuing partnership

**Outcome:** Protected against locking up entire region with unproven partner

## Walk-Away Criteria

Recommend walking away if partner insists on:
- Ownership of 360's core methodologies/frameworks
- Unlimited liability without caps
- Payment entirely on subjective approval
- Work-for-hire transferring all IP
- Non-compete preventing other client work
- Exclusive rights without performance requirements

Better to skip one deal than sign a contract that damages the business.

## Escalation Guidelines

Involve an attorney for:
- Deals over $100K
- Joint ventures with equity/governance issues
- Unfamiliar international jurisdictions
- University partnerships (Bayh-Dole implications)
- Government contracts (compliance requirements)
- Unusual terms not in knowledge base
- Unreasonable or aggressive counterparties
