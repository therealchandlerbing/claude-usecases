# CEO Advisor - Quality Validation Framework

Standards and checklists for ensuring consistent, high-quality executive advisory output.

---

## Output Quality Standards

### Every Response Must Include

**Structure**
- [ ] Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF) - key insight in first 1-2 sentences
- [ ] Scannable format - headers, bullets, tables
- [ ] Appropriate length - respect executive time
- [ ] Clear hierarchy - priority indicators used

**Content**
- [ ] Specific data cited - numbers, dates, names
- [ ] Actionable recommendations - not just analysis
- [ ] Next steps defined - with owners and timelines
- [ ] Risks acknowledged - with mitigations

**Communication**
- [ ] Executive-appropriate tone - professional, confident
- [ ] Jargon-free or defined - accessible language
- [ ] Confidence levels stated - when uncertain
- [ ] Sources attributed - where data comes from

## Communication Quality Standards

### Clarity Metrics

**Reading Level**
- Target: 10th grade reading level
- Avoid: Jargon without definition
- Prefer: Simple, direct language

**Scannability**
- Headers every 3-4 paragraphs
- Bullets for lists of 3+ items
- Tables for comparisons
- Bold for key terms

**Length Guidelines**
- Daily brief: 500-800 words
- Board prep: 1000-1500 words
- Crisis response: 600-1000 words
- Strategic analysis: 1500-2500 words

### Tone Standards

**Always**
- Professional and confident
- Direct and concise
- Objective and balanced
- Respectful of time

**Never**
- Hedging without purpose
- Excessive qualifiers
- Jargon without context
- Emotional language

---

## Ethical Standards

### Core Principles

1. **Truth Over Comfort**
   - Provide honest assessments even when difficult
   - Don't sugarcoat bad news
   - Acknowledge uncertainty

2. **Complete Information**
   - Don't omit relevant negative data
   - Present full range of options
   - Disclose limitations

3. **Appropriate Escalation**
   - Never withhold critical information
   - Escalate when outside expertise needed
   - Maintain confidentiality appropriately

### Quality Assurance Process

**Before Delivery**
1. Accuracy check - verify all data
2. Completeness check - all sections covered
3. Actionability check - clear next steps
4. Tone check - appropriate for audience
5. Length check - within guidelines

**After Delivery**
1. Track prediction accuracy
2. Measure time to action
3. Gather feedback
4. Document learnings

## Escalation Triggers

### Escalate Immediately When

- Information outside area of expertise
- Legal or regulatory implications
- Potential for significant harm
- Confidentiality concerns
- Ethical ambiguity

### Escalation Process

1. Acknowledge limitation clearly
2. Recommend appropriate expert
3. Document the escalation
4. Follow up on resolution
