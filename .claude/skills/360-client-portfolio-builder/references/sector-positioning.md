# Sector Positioning - 360 Client Portfolio Builder

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-16
**Purpose:** Industry-specific framing, language, and positioning guidance

## Overview

Every sector has its own language, stakeholders, and success metrics. This guide helps you adapt portfolio content to resonate with sector-specific audiences while maintaining clarity for general stakeholders.

**Key Principle:** Speak the sector's language without losing accessibility.

## CleanTech / Climate Tech

### Sector Context

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Impact investors focused on climate
- Corporate sustainability officers
- Government environmental agencies
- International development organizations
- Climate funds and green banks

**What They Care About:**
- Carbon emissions avoided or reduced
- Climate resilience and adaptation
- Scalability of climate impact
- Policy alignment (Paris Agreement, SDG 13)
- Transition to renewable energy

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Carbon footprint / emissions reduction
- Renewable energy / clean energy
- Net zero / carbon neutral
- Climate resilience / adaptation
- Energy access / energy poverty
- Circular economy / waste reduction

**Framing Patterns:**

Instead of generic impact:
> "We're making energy more accessible."

Use climate-specific framing:
> "We're accelerating the clean energy transition by making solar power the default choice for 840M people currently dependent on fossil fuels."

Instead of vague sustainability:
> "Our solution is environmentally friendly."

Use specific metrics:
> "Each micro-grid avoids 15 tons of CO2 annually compared to diesel generators—equivalent to taking 3 cars off the road per installation."

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Deep green (#047857) - environmental, growth
**Secondary:** Sky blue (#0EA5E9) - clean energy, water
**Accent:** Earth tones (terracotta, sand) - natural, grounded

### Metrics to Emphasize

**Environmental Impact:**
- CO2 emissions avoided (tons)
- Clean energy delivered (MWh, kWh)
- Fossil fuel displaced (liters diesel, tons coal)
- Waste diverted from landfills (tons)
- Water saved or cleaned (liters, m³)

**Systems Change:**
- Households/communities with clean energy access
- Hectares restored or protected
- Policy changes influenced
- Industry adoption of cleaner practices

**Example Traction Section:**

```markdown
## Climate Impact

**Emissions Reduction:**
- 180 tons of CO2 avoided annually
- Equivalent to removing 38 cars from roads
- 450 MWh of clean energy delivered

**Energy Access:**
- 5,200 families transitioned from diesel to solar
- 98% uptime vs. 60% for diesel generators
- $840K saved by communities in fuel costs
```

### Positioning by Sub-Sector

**Renewable Energy:**
- Emphasize grid integration and reliability
- Show cost parity or advantage vs. fossil fuels
- Highlight energy access for underserved markets

**Circular Economy:**
- Focus on waste reduction and resource efficiency
- Show economic value from "waste" streams
- Emphasize scalability and systems change

**Climate Adaptation:**
- Lead with resilience and risk reduction
- Show benefit to vulnerable communities
- Connect to climate science and projections

## HealthTech / Digital Health

### Sector Context

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Health systems and hospital networks
- Public health agencies
- Health-focused foundations
- Pharma and medical device companies
- Health insurance providers

**What They Care About:**
- Clinical validation and evidence
- Regulatory compliance (FDA, CE mark)
- Patient outcomes and safety
- Healthcare cost reduction
- Health equity and access

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Clinical validation / evidence-based
- Patient outcomes / health outcomes
- Diagnostic accuracy / sensitivity/specificity
- Care coordination / continuity of care
- Health equity / access to care
- Telehealth / remote monitoring

**Framing Patterns:**

Instead of generic health:
> "We're improving healthcare access."

Use specific clinical framing:
> "We're expanding specialist access to rural clinics by providing AI-powered diagnostic support, reducing referral delays from 6 weeks to 48 hours."

Instead of vague outcomes:
> "Patients get better care."

Use clinical evidence:
> "Clinical pilots show 94% diagnostic accuracy for pneumonia detection, validated against radiologist consensus. This enables earlier treatment initiation in resource-limited settings."

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Medical blue (#3B82F6) - trust, clinical
**Secondary:** Coral (#FB7185) - warm, human-centered
**Accent:** Teal (#14B8A6) - healing, vitality

### Metrics to Emphasize

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- Diagnostic accuracy (sensitivity, specificity)
- Time to treatment / diagnosis
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Adverse event reduction
- Clinical validation study results

**Access & Equity:**
- Patients served (especially underserved)
- Geographic reach (rural, low-resource)
- Cost reduction for patients or systems
- Wait time reduction

**Example Traction Section:**

```markdown
## Clinical Validation & Impact

**Diagnostic Performance:**
- 94% sensitivity for pneumonia detection
- 96% specificity (validated against 3 radiologists)
- Published in *Journal of Global Health*

**Patient Access:**
- 12 rural clinics connected to specialist network
- Reduced referral wait time from 6 weeks to 48 hours
- Served 2,400 patients in underserved regions

**System Impact:**
- 30% reduction in unnecessary hospital transfers
- $180K saved in transportation costs
```

### Positioning by Sub-Sector

**Diagnostics:**
- Lead with clinical validation and accuracy
- Show comparison to gold standard
- Emphasize accessibility for resource-limited settings

**Telehealth:**
- Focus on care continuity and patient experience
- Show outcomes vs. in-person care
- Highlight regulatory compliance

**Health Systems:**
- Emphasize workflow integration
- Show cost-effectiveness and efficiency
- Focus on provider satisfaction

## EdTech / Learning Technology

### Sector Context

**Key Stakeholders:**
- School districts and education ministries
- Foundations focused on education
- EdTech investors
- Teachers and school administrators
- Parents and families

**What They Care About:**
- Learning outcomes and assessment
- Teacher effectiveness and satisfaction
- Scalability across diverse contexts
- Cost-effectiveness vs. traditional methods
- Equity and access for underserved students

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Learning outcomes / academic achievement
- Personalized / adaptive learning
- Student engagement / motivation
- Teacher professional development
- Education equity / opportunity gap
- Literacy / numeracy

**Framing Patterns:**

Instead of generic education:
> "We're making learning more effective."

Use outcomes-focused framing:
> "We're closing the achievement gap for underserved students through adaptive learning that meets each child where they are, delivering 1.5 years of growth in a single school year."

Instead of vague technology:
> "We use AI to improve education."

Use specific pedagogical value:
> "Our platform identifies exactly where each student struggles and provides targeted practice at the right difficulty level, freeing teachers to focus on high-impact one-on-one support."

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Purple (#9333EA) - creativity, learning
**Secondary:** Warm yellow (#F59E0B) - optimism, growth
**Accent:** Teal (#14B8A6) - engagement, vitality

### Metrics to Emphasize

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Test score improvements
- Grade-level equivalents gained
- Proficiency rate changes
- Engagement time / completion rates

**Teacher Impact:**
- Time saved on grading/admin
- Teacher satisfaction scores
- Professional development completion

**Access & Equity:**
- Students served (especially underserved)
- Schools/districts using platform
- Cost per student vs. alternatives
- Internet connectivity solutions

**Example Traction Section:**

```markdown
## Learning Impact

**Student Outcomes:**
- 1.5 grade levels gained in math (on average)
- 78% of students reached proficiency (vs. 45% baseline)
- 85% student engagement rate

**Teacher Impact:**
- 6 hours per week saved on assessment and grading
- 92% teacher satisfaction score
- 240 teachers trained across 18 schools

**Reach & Equity:**
- 8,400 students in underserved communities
- 12 rural school districts across 3 states
- Works offline for low-connectivity environments
```

### Positioning by Sub-Sector

**K-12 Education:**
- Emphasize alignment with standards
- Show teacher and student outcomes
- Focus on classroom integration

**Higher Education:**
- Highlight skill development for employment
- Show completion and retention rates
- Focus on affordability

**Workforce Development:**
- Emphasize job placement and salary outcomes
- Show industry alignment and partnerships
- Focus on underserved populations

## FinTech / Financial Inclusion

### Sector Context

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Impact investors focused on financial inclusion
- Microfinance institutions
- Banks and financial services companies
- Regulators and central banks
- Development finance institutions

**What They Care About:**
- Financial inclusion metrics
- Regulatory compliance and licensing
- Credit risk and default rates
- Transaction volumes and costs
- Financial literacy and capability

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Financial inclusion / underbanked
- Microfinance / microcredit
- Mobile money / digital payments
- Credit scoring / alternative data
- Remittances / cross-border payments
- Financial literacy / capability

**Framing Patterns:**

Instead of generic access:
> "We're making finance more accessible."

Use inclusion-focused framing:
> "We're extending credit to 2M underbanked entrepreneurs using alternative data for scoring, enabling business growth for those excluded by traditional banks."

Instead of vague technology:
> "We use AI for better lending."

Use specific risk and impact framing:
> "Our machine learning models use mobile phone and business transaction data to assess creditworthiness with 8% default rates—comparable to traditional credit bureaus while serving markets they ignore."

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Navy (#1E3A8A) - trust, stability
**Secondary:** Gold (#D97706) - value, prosperity
**Accent:** Green (#10B981) - growth, success

### Metrics to Emphasize

**Financial Inclusion:**
- Users served (especially unbanked/underbanked)
- First-time access to credit/savings
- Geographic reach in underserved markets
- Cost reduction vs. alternatives

**Financial Performance:**
- Transaction volumes and values
- Default rates / loan performance
- Customer lifetime value
- Operational efficiency metrics

**Example Traction Section:**

```markdown
## Financial Inclusion Impact

**Reach:**
- 2.1M underbanked users served
- 65% first-time access to formal credit
- Operating in 4 countries across East Africa

**Financial Performance:**
- $48M in loans disbursed
- 8% default rate (industry average: 12%)
- $2.4M monthly transaction volume

**Impact:**
- Average business revenue increase: 35%
- 78% of borrowers took second loan (strong retention)
- $180 average loan size (designed for micro-entrepreneurs)
```

### Positioning by Sub-Sector

**Payments:**
- Emphasize speed and cost vs. traditional methods
- Show transaction security and fraud prevention
- Focus on cross-border and remittance use cases

**Lending:**
- Lead with credit model innovation and default rates
- Show financial inclusion metrics
- Emphasize borrower success and repayment

**Savings / Insurance:**
- Focus on financial resilience and security
- Show adoption and retention rates
- Emphasize protection for vulnerable populations

## AgTech / Agricultural Technology

### Sector Context

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Agricultural development organizations
- Food security foundations
- Agribusiness companies
- Government agriculture ministries
- Smallholder farmer cooperatives

**What They Care About:**
- Farmer income and livelihood improvement
- Crop yield increases
- Climate resilience and adaptation
- Food security and nutrition
- Sustainable farming practices

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Smallholder farmers / agricultural productivity
- Crop yield / harvest quality
- Climate-smart agriculture
- Food security / nutrition security
- Agricultural extension / farmer training
- Sustainable / regenerative agriculture

**Framing Patterns:**

Instead of generic farming:
> "We're helping farmers grow more."

Use livelihood and resilience framing:
> "We're increasing smallholder farmer incomes by 45% through precision agriculture tools that optimize water and fertilizer use, while building climate resilience."

Instead of vague technology:
> "We use sensors for better farming."

Use specific agricultural value:
> "Our soil sensors and mobile app tell farmers exactly when to irrigate and fertilize, reducing water use by 30% and increasing yields by 25% even during drought conditions."

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Agricultural green (#16A34A) - growth, crops
**Secondary:** Earth brown (#92400E) - soil, grounded
**Accent:** Sunshine yellow (#EAB308) - optimism, harvest

### Metrics to Emphasize

**Farmer Impact:**
- Income increase (% or absolute)
- Yield increase (tons/hectare, %)
- Cost reduction (inputs, labor)
- Time saved

**Environmental:**
- Water saved
- Fertilizer/pesticide reduction
- Carbon sequestration
- Soil health improvement

**Example Traction Section:**

```markdown
## Farmer Impact

**Economic Outcomes:**
- 45% average income increase for participating farmers
- 25% crop yield improvement
- 30% reduction in water usage
- 20% reduction in fertilizer costs

**Reach:**
- 12,000 smallholder farmers across 3 countries
- 85% retention rate (farmers continuing after first season)
- 18,000 hectares under climate-smart practices

**Climate Resilience:**
- Maintained yields during 2024 drought (neighbors saw 40% loss)
- 15 tons CO2e sequestered per hectare annually
```

## General Social Innovation

### When to Use

Use this framing when:
- Client doesn't fit neatly into one sector
- Work spans multiple sectors
- Social systems change is primary focus
- Target audience is cross-sector

### Language & Framing

**Key Terms:**
- Systems change / social innovation
- Community-driven / participatory
- Social impact / collective impact
- Equity / justice / inclusion
- Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- Co-creation / partnership

**Framing Patterns:**

Lead with systems change:
> "We're addressing the root causes of [problem] by [approach] that shifts how [system] operates."

Emphasize collaboration:
> "In partnership with local communities and [stakeholders], we're co-creating solutions that build on existing strengths while introducing new capabilities."

Connect to broader movements:
> "This work contributes to [SDG / movement / sector transformation] by demonstrating that [new approach] can work at scale."

### Color Palette

Use 360 brand colors:
**Primary:** Sage (#87A878) - 360 primary
**Secondary:** Terracotta (#C97A63) - 360 secondary
**Accent:** Plum (#6B5B95) - 360 tertiary

## Cross-Sector Positioning Tips

### When Client Spans Multiple Sectors

**Strategy 1: Lead with Primary, Acknowledge Secondary**

Example (HealthTech + Climate):
> "HealthBridge improves health outcomes in climate-vulnerable communities while reducing healthcare's carbon footprint through telemedicine, eliminating 1,200 tons of CO2 from patient travel annually."

**Strategy 2: Frame as Integrated Impact**

Example (EdTech + Economic Development):
> "SkillBridge prepares underemployed adults for green economy jobs, connecting workforce development with climate action."

**Strategy 3: Use Systems Change Framing**

Example (Multi-sector):
> "Our approach addresses interconnected challenges—health, education, and economic opportunity—recognizing that sustainable change requires holistic solutions."

### Adapting for International Audiences

**For Latin America:**
- Emphasize relationship-building and community
- Highlight partnership and collaboration
- Use warmer, more personal tone
- See [cultural-intelligence.md](cultural-intelligence.md) for details

**For European Stakeholders:**
- Lead with data and evidence
- Emphasize regulatory compliance and standards
- Show policy alignment (EU frameworks)
- Focus on sustainability and long-term thinking

**For U.S. Investors:**
- Emphasize scalability and market size
- Show clear path to sustainability or profitability
- Highlight innovation and competitive advantage
- Include financial metrics prominently

## Anti-Patterns by Sector

### CleanTech: What to Avoid

❌ Vague environmental claims without data
❌ Greenwashing (overstating climate impact)
❌ Ignoring policy/regulatory context
❌ Unrealistic timelines for climate impact

### HealthTech: What to Avoid

❌ Claiming clinical benefit without validation
❌ Downplaying regulatory requirements
❌ Overpromising on diagnostic accuracy
❌ Ignoring patient privacy and data security

### EdTech: What to Avoid

❌ Focusing on technology over learning outcomes
❌ Ignoring teacher experience and buy-in
❌ Claiming to "replace" teachers
❌ One-size-fits-all solutions

### FinTech: What to Avoid

❌ Underestimating regulatory complexity
❌ Ignoring financial literacy of users
❌ Overpromising returns or understating risk
❌ Exploitative pricing for underserved markets
