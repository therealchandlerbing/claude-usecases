## First-Time Review Checklist

When you open your first brief, check:

- [ ] Does the executive summary highlight the most important developments?
- [ ] Are all major partnerships represented?
- [ ] Is team composition accurate?
- [ ] Do the visualizations show correct data?
- [ ] Are action items assigned to the right people?
- [ ] Is the timeline complete with upcoming events?

If anything seems off, ask Claude to refine specific sections.

## Common First-Time Issues

**Issue**: Brief feels incomplete or generic
**Fix**: Make sure your Asana projects have recent updates and Google Drive has current strategic documents

**Issue**: Missing specific partnership information
**Fix**: Check that partnership emails are in Gmail and partnership names are searchable

**Issue**: Charts show placeholder data
**Fix**: Verify that Asana custom fields are populated (Priority, Revenue Impact, Partnership Type)

**Issue**: Action items seem random
**Fix**: Review Asana tasks to ensure high-priority items have clear owners and due dates

## Tips for Best Results

**Be Specific About Context**: Mention if you're preparing for a board meeting, investor call, or strategy session. This helps prioritize relevant information.

**Provide Date Ranges**: Default is the past week, but specify other ranges when needed ("past two weeks," "month of November").

**Request Iterations**: Don't settle for the first draft. Ask Claude to expand sections, add detail, or refine specific content.

**Keep Data Current**: The brief quality depends on your operational data. Regular Asana updates and organized email make a big difference.

**Review Before Sharing**: Always review for accuracy before distributing to stakeholders. This is intelligence synthesis, not a fully automated report.
