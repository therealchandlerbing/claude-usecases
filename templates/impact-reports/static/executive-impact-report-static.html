EVALUATION APPROACH

METHODOLOGY

  • Common outcome framework

    Shared indicators and definitions across programs enable more meaningful comparison of impact and resource allocation decisions.

  • Mixed methods evaluation

    Quantitative data is paired with qualitative insight from communities and partners to avoid purely top-down views and capture nuance.

  • Learning loops

    Insights from pilots feed into portfolio decisions, venture support, and future program designs on an ongoing basis rather than annual cycles.

  • External validation

    Independent evaluators review methodology and findings to ensure rigor and credibility with stakeholders.

Next 18 months

Focused investment in deepening existing work rather than broadening footprint, with emphasis on Brazil hub, health-climate intersections, and digital public goods.

HEALTH & CLIMATE

  • Coastal communities

    Integrated programs addressing climate resilience, health access, and livelihood security in vulnerable coastal regions.

  • Rural adaptation

    Support farming communities with climate-smart agriculture, health services, and economic diversification.

  • Data and early warning

    Deploy digital tools that connect climate data, health surveillance, and community preparedness systems.

DIGITAL PUBLIC GOODS

  • Workforce platforms

    Open-source curricula and certification systems for health, tech, and climate roles that can travel across partners.

  • Telehealth infrastructure

    Reusable tools for remote diagnosis, patient monitoring, and data interoperability in low-resource contexts.

  • Data standards

    Support adoption of common data formats and APIs that reduce vendor lock-in and enable ecosystem collaboration.

STRATEGIC OUTLOOK

What we need from leadership

A small number of board and executive decisions will have disproportionate impact on our next chapter and ability to scale proven models.

ROADMAP HORIZONS

6 TO 36 MONTHS

  • Horizon 1 (Next 6 months)

    Consolidate early wins in Brazil and other anchor geographies, finalize lab and hub structures, and standardize how we measure outcomes across partners.

  • Horizon 2 (6 to 18 months)

    Scale proven models with priority partners, expand digital public goods, and deepen the connection between climate, health, and livelihoods work.

  • Horizon 3 (18 to 36 months)

    Position 360 Social Impact Studios as a leading innovation partner across the Global South, with a portfolio of scalable ventures and robust, long-term ecosystem relationships.

LEADERSHIP ASKS

CRITICAL DECISIONS

  • Confirm investment envelope and risk appetite

    Clarify how much capital, and at what level of risk tolerance, can be deployed to early-stage and blended structures over the next 24 months.

  • Endorse priority partnerships

    Confirm which 3-5 strategic partnerships are most important to advance, and where CEO/board attention and relationship capital should be focused.

  • Invest in internal capacity

    Strengthen core team capacity in innovation management, partnership stewardship, and data infrastructure to support 2x growth without compromising quality.

  • Approve Brazil hub investment

    Authorize dedicated resources for establishing permanent presence, hiring local leadership, and building regional partnerships over 18 months.
